U.S. Gasoline Prices Hit Politically Sensitive $4 Level As Trump Eyes Iran War Off-Ramp

The overnight Wall Street Journal report that President Trump told aides he is [willing to wind down](https://www.zerohedge.com/geopolitical/trump-cites-progress-dealing-more-reasonable-regime-while-mulling-ground-operation) the U.S. military campaign against Iran even if the Strait of Hormuz remains disrupted ([_and appeared to confirm this narrative in a social media post this morning_](https://cms.zerohedge.com/markets/ramp-imminent-trump-tells-world-go-get-your-own-oil-strait-after-decimating-iran)) comes just as the national average gasoline price hit the politically sensitive $4-a-gallon threshold, underscoring the delicate balancing act the administration is facing in managing battlefield objectives and domestic fuel costs.

The latest [AAA](https://gasprices.aaa.com/) data shows gasoline prices nationwide topped $4 a gallon on Monday, a 35% increase for Regular 87 at the pump and the largest price shock on record dating back to 2004.

[![](https://assets.zerohedge.com/s3fs-public/styles/inline_image_mobile/public/inline-images/2026-03-31_07-31-06.png?itok=warZIIOo)](https://cms.zerohedge.com/s3/files/inline-images/2026-03-31_07-31-06.png?itok=warZIIOo)

Regular 87 gasoline prices at the pump nationwide have returned to the price shock levels seen during the 2022 Russia-Ukraine crisis.

[![](https://assets.zerohedge.com/s3fs-public/styles/inline_image_mobile/public/inline-images/Snag_bac7a79.png?itok=dpFoAvu9)](https://cms.zerohedge.com/s3/files/inline-images/Snag_bac7a79.png?itok=dpFoAvu9)

Largest monthly price shock on record.

[![](https://assets.zerohedge.com/s3fs-public/styles/inline_image_mobile/public/inline-images/Snag_bac57ce.png?itok=Vti7wpMt)](https://cms.zerohedge.com/s3/files/inline-images/Snag_bac57ce.png?itok=Vti7wpMt)

Early last week, Bonnie Herzog, managing director and senior consumer analyst at Goldman Sachs, wrote in a note that when fuel prices spike to these "[psychological threshold](https://www.zerohedge.com/energy/nearing-psychological-gas-price-level-where-consumers-drive-less)" levels, above $3 and approaching $4 a gallon, consumers tend to drive less and fill up their tanks less frequently.

"**Historically, when retail gas prices increase (especially above the $3/gal psychological threshold, although that's been rebased higher), consumers make the concerted decision to drive less, don't always fill up their tanks (i.e., lower fill rates),"** Herzog told clients.

[![](https://assets.zerohedge.com/s3fs-public/styles/inline_image_mobile/public/inline-images/2026-03-23_08-50-52.png?itok=xfDNLwFn)](https://cms.zerohedge.com/s3/files/inline-images/2026-03-23_08-50-52.png?itok=xfDNLwFn)

But Herzog pointed back to history, noting that the real demand destruction for drivers comes when gasoline prices at the pump reach $5 a gallon.

She noted, "**Further, we recognize that, in times of a significantly rising fuel-price environment, consumers may opt to trade down the fuel-price spectrum (i.e., from premium to regular)**."

Furthermore, AAA data shows the national average diesel price has surged 45% this month to $5.45 per gallon. That marks the largest spike on record.

[![](https://assets.zerohedge.com/s3fs-public/styles/inline_image_mobile/public/inline-images/Snag_bac392b.png?itok=1yTqS_dz)](https://cms.zerohedge.com/s3/files/inline-images/Snag_bac392b.png?itok=1yTqS_dz)

The price shock is already [sending shockwaves](https://www.zerohedge.com/energy/going-cripple-our-economy-small-businesses-sound-alarm-over-record-diesel-price-spike) through the real economy. Diesel powers the industrial backbone of the nation: trucking fleets, rail networks, shipping, farm equipment, construction machinery, backup generators, and broad segments of heavy logistics. When diesel prices spike this quickly, cost shock hits companies at the pump, with logistics firms [passing fuel surcharges on to customers](https://www.zerohedge.com/economics/us-postal-service-plans-8-fuel-surcharge-iran-war-raises-transport-costs).

We warned readers on Monday about the unfolding "[global demand destruction](https://www.zerohedge.com/markets/global-demand-destruction-subsidies-empty-gas-stations-rationing-flight-cancelations-export)" and noted that the energy shock was already beginning to ripple outward from Asia.
