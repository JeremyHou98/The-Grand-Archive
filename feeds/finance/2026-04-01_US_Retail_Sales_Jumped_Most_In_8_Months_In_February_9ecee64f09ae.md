US Retail Sales Jumped Most In 8 Months In February

Bank of America's omniscient analysts forecast a very strong month for Retail Sales in February data (released today)...

[![](https://assets.zerohedge.com/s3fs-public/styles/inline_image_mobile/public/inline-images/get_attachment_url%20%2852%29_3.jpg?itok=glC64Cl3)](https://cms.zerohedge.com/s3/files/inline-images/get_attachment_url%20%2852%29_3.jpg?itok=glC64Cl3)

The actual print was +0.6% MoM (better than the 0.5% consensus, but less than BofA's forecast) comes after a revised higher 0.1% MoM decline in January (and December's nothingburger)...

[![](https://assets.zerohedge.com/s3fs-public/styles/inline_image_mobile/public/inline-images/bfm75BA.jpg?itok=m9GuV1rB)](https://cms.zerohedge.com/s3/files/inline-images/bfm75BA.jpg?itok=m9GuV1rB)

Source: Bloomberg

**That is the highest MoM rise sinc****e June 2025, and sales rose 3.7% YoY...**

[![](https://assets.zerohedge.com/s3fs-public/styles/inline_image_mobile/public/inline-images/bfmDF1A_0.jpg?itok=IAoh-KbV)](https://cms.zerohedge.com/s3/files/inline-images/bfmDF1A_0.jpg?itok=IAoh-KbV)

Core Retail Sales (Ex Autos) rose 0.5% MoM (much better than expected) and Ex Autos and Gas also rose more than expected (+0.4% MoM).

**Food and Beverage spending fell while Motor Vehicle and Parts Dealers saw the biggest jump...**

[![](https://assets.zerohedge.com/s3fs-public/styles/inline_image_mobile/public/inline-images/bfmE63.jpg?itok=TwmOrFo2)](https://cms.zerohedge.com/s3/files/inline-images/bfmE63.jpg?itok=TwmOrFo2)

Most importantly, **the 'Control Group' which plugs into the GDP calculation rose 0.5% MoM (also considerably better than expected)**.

[![](https://assets.zerohedge.com/s3fs-public/styles/inline_image_mobile/public/inline-images/bfm4A0C.jpg?itok=lZhEkA7K)](https://cms.zerohedge.com/s3/files/inline-images/bfm4A0C.jpg?itok=lZhEkA7K)

Interestingly, 'real' retail sales (admittedly crudely adjusted via CPI) have rebounded from a negative print in December...

[![](https://assets.zerohedge.com/s3fs-public/styles/inline_image_mobile/public/inline-images/bfmF85E.jpg?itok=IiMiWJN-)](https://cms.zerohedge.com/s3/files/inline-images/bfmF85E.jpg?itok=IiMiWJN-)

Of course, this data was before the war started and before gas prices really exploded (but then again April's tax refunds may offset some of the pain).
