Savings Rate Slides As Fed's Favorite Inflation Gauge Slowed In February (Ahead Of War)

The **Fed's favorite inflation indicator** \- Core PCE (a measure of price changes in consumer goods and services that excludes volatile food and energy costs) - rose 0.4% MoM in February (pre-war), in line with expectations, with YoY rising 3.0% (as expected - lowest since Dec), down from January's +3.1%...

[![](https://assets.zerohedge.com/s3fs-public/styles/inline_image_mobile/public/inline-images/bfm2223_0.jpg?itok=E8qJmWeL)](https://cms.zerohedge.com/s3/files/inline-images/bfm2223_0.jpg?itok=E8qJmWeL)

Source: Bloomberg

The YoY Core decline is coming off January's highest level since March 2024, with Services cost inflation slowing notably...

[![](https://assets.zerohedge.com/s3fs-public/styles/inline_image_mobile/public/inline-images/get_attachment_url%20%281%29_14.jpg?itok=uSVsbZaM)](https://cms.zerohedge.com/s3/files/inline-images/get_attachment_url%20%281%29_14.jpg?itok=uSVsbZaM)

The headline PCE also rose 0.4% MoM (as expected - the **biggest MoM rise since Feb 2025**), up 2.8% YoY (also as expected)...

[![](https://assets.zerohedge.com/s3fs-public/styles/inline_image_mobile/public/inline-images/bfmA005_0.jpg?itok=zPPmhbK2)](https://cms.zerohedge.com/s3/files/inline-images/bfmA005_0.jpg?itok=zPPmhbK2)

Source: Bloomberg

Under the hood, we saw a notable jump in non-durable goods prices...

[![](https://assets.zerohedge.com/s3fs-public/styles/inline_image_mobile/public/inline-images/get_attachment_url_11.jpg?itok=42WG-ahr)](https://cms.zerohedge.com/s3/files/inline-images/get_attachment_url_11.jpg?itok=42WG-ahr)

Source: Bloomberg

The much-watched SuperCore PCE (Services Ex-Shelter rose 0.2% MoM with the YoY rise tumbling to +3.2% - in line with its lowest level since March 2021...

[![](https://assets.zerohedge.com/s3fs-public/styles/inline_image_mobile/public/inline-images/bfmC51F_0.jpg?itok=HueKDEpz)](https://cms.zerohedge.com/s3/files/inline-images/bfmC51F_0.jpg?itok=HueKDEpz)

Under the hood, Recreation Services and Healthcare saw the largest deceleration MoM...

[![](https://assets.zerohedge.com/s3fs-public/styles/inline_image_mobile/public/inline-images/get_attachment_url%20%283%29_7.jpg?itok=Qq8OWA2c)](https://cms.zerohedge.com/s3/files/inline-images/get_attachment_url%20%283%29_7.jpg?itok=Qq8OWA2c)

For those worried about the impact of crude oil's recent surge (since the start of the Iran war), it appears - somehow - that PCE's Energy component has already front-run some of the move but there's a lot more pain to come for March...

[![](https://assets.zerohedge.com/s3fs-public/styles/inline_image_mobile/public/inline-images/bfm9723.jpg?itok=GVYZc11H)](https://cms.zerohedge.com/s3/files/inline-images/bfm9723.jpg?itok=GVYZc11H)

Source: Bloomberg

Higher prices were met with **lower incomes (-0.1% MoM vs +0.3% MoM exp) and higher spending** (+90.5% MoM vs +0.6% MoM exp)...

[![](https://assets.zerohedge.com/s3fs-public/styles/inline_image_mobile/public/inline-images/bfm611.jpg?itok=ACnHTRZm)](https://cms.zerohedge.com/s3/files/inline-images/bfm611.jpg?itok=ACnHTRZm)

Source: Bloomberg

Income growth is slowing significantly while spending is accelerating...

[![](https://assets.zerohedge.com/s3fs-public/styles/inline_image_mobile/public/inline-images/bfmC309.jpg?itok=7bMHXZdF)](https://cms.zerohedge.com/s3/files/inline-images/bfmC309.jpg?itok=7bMHXZdF)

Source: Bloomberg

Adjusted for inflation, **real spending rose 2.5% YoY - the highest since Oct 2025**...

[![](https://assets.zerohedge.com/s3fs-public/styles/inline_image_mobile/public/inline-images/bfmF455.jpg?itok=_4gIqdKQ)](https://cms.zerohedge.com/s3/files/inline-images/bfmF455.jpg?itok=_4gIqdKQ)

Source: Bloomberg

After jumping from 3.9% to 4.5% in January, **Americans' savings rate dropped back to 4.0%** in Feb (after another huge revision in late 2025), **basically at the weakest level since Nov 2023...**

[![](https://assets.zerohedge.com/s3fs-public/styles/inline_image_mobile/public/inline-images/get_attachment_url%20%282%29_10.jpg?itok=rJDx1aDH)](https://cms.zerohedge.com/s3/files/inline-images/get_attachment_url%20%282%29_10.jpg?itok=rJDx1aDH)

Source: Bloomberg

So spending solid as incomes fell and prices are rising... but this is all pre-war, so a large pinch of salt is required.
