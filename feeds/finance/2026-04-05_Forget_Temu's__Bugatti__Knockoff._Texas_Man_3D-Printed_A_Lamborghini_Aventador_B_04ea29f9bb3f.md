Forget Temu's "Bugatti" Knockoff. Texas Man 3D-Printed A Lamborghini Aventador Body

Forget ordering a $30,000 "Bugatti" knockoff from Chinese e-commerce websites like Temu.

A private seller in Texas is now offering what appears to be a fully 3D-printed Lamborghini Aventador body on Facebook Marketplace, highlighting how 3D printing is revolutionizing custom vehicle manufacturing.

[![](https://assets.zerohedge.com/s3fs-public/styles/inline_image_mobile/public/inline-images/647210674_1270438678487598_7225328542821656215_n.jpg?itok=ORi2qGq5)](https://cms.zerohedge.com/s3/files/inline-images/647210674_1270438678487598_7225328542821656215_n.jpg?itok=ORi2qGq5)

"This is a fully 3D-printed Lamborghini Aventador project that gives you a huge head start. It includes the complete body, front frame, rear frame, and monocoque already printed and sized to Aventador dimensions," the listing stated.

[![](https://assets.zerohedge.com/s3fs-public/styles/inline_image_mobile/public/inline-images/648733831_1451450423195753_1323110046667934850_n.jpg?itok=fFCWL-9q)](https://cms.zerohedge.com/s3/files/inline-images/648733831_1451450423195753_1323110046667934850_n.jpg?itok=fFCWL-9q)

The 3D-printed Aventador body is listed for $5,000. But the price jumps to $7,500 if buyers want the exterior and interior all glued together, or $8,500 if they want the frame pieces included in the gluing.

[![](https://assets.zerohedge.com/s3fs-public/styles/inline_image_mobile/public/inline-images/649974819_1463495878463052_1724160808115385509_n.jpg?itok=uR2SzQKK)](https://cms.zerohedge.com/s3/files/inline-images/649974819_1463495878463052_1724160808115385509_n.jpg?itok=uR2SzQKK)

To complete the build, the seller says the body will still need to be reinforced with fiberglass, mounted to a steel frame, and fitted with a drivetrain, suspension, and interior ([view listing here](https://www.facebook.com/marketplace/item/1211852070985720)). 

[![](https://assets.zerohedge.com/s3fs-public/styles/inline_image_mobile/public/inline-images/646420550_1595467705228582_8570004924075563230_n.jpg?itok=dNOpWwOL)](https://cms.zerohedge.com/s3/files/inline-images/646420550_1595467705228582_8570004924075563230_n.jpg?itok=dNOpWwOL)

Automotive website [Jalopnik](https://www.jalopnik.com/2132370/marketplace-seller-is-listing-a-sketchy-3d-printed-lamborghini-aventador/?utm_term=Autofeed&utm_campaign=Echobox-Jalopnik&utm_medium=Social-Distribution&utm_source=Facebook&fbclid=IwY2xjawQ15SlleHRuA2FlbQIxMABicmlkETFLMEpQNG9mZTNSalZjWjJSc3J0YwZhcHBfaWQQMjIyMDM5MTc4ODIwMDg5MgABHjqo7mXXrFbmisoY0Bjy8RGzsAwsbtl006troCGR9qUl9jGXcnITP7nhyGOk_aem_C_S3w48ufXFbbNqIof4dtQ#Echobox=1774701105) was the first to report the listing, offering its take:

> _I may have some ideas about 3D print strength that friends of mine call "overly conservative" or "downright anxious," but I still don't think I'd trust a car with a tub that's been glued together out of various 3D prints. The seller doesn't even specify what kind of plastic they're using. ABS is an option, but ever-popular PLA filament will degrade under the kind of constant UV exposure that a car sees._

Well, this certainly beats the ["Bugatti" knockoff from Temu](https://www.zerohedge.com/technology/i-bought-bugatti-temu).
